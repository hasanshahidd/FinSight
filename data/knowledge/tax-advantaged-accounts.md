# Tax-Advantaged Accounts: 401(k), IRA, HSA Explained

The US tax code rewards retirement saving with three main account types. Using them in the right order can shift tens of thousands of dollars from the IRS to your future self.

## 401(k) - workplace retirement plan

- **Pre-tax (Traditional)**: contributions reduce current taxable income; growth is tax-deferred; withdrawals taxed as ordinary income.
- **Roth**: contributions made with after-tax money; growth and qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
- **2024 limit**: $23,000 (+$7,500 catch-up if 50+).
- **Employer match**: typically 50–100% of your first 3–6%. **This is free money - always capture it before any other investing.**
- **Vesting**: employer contributions may not be 100% yours immediately; check the schedule.

## IRA - individual retirement account

- Open it yourself (Fidelity, Vanguard, Schwab - no advisor required).
- **2024 limit**: $7,000 (+$1,000 catch-up).
- **Traditional IRA**: deductible if income below thresholds; same tax treatment as Traditional 401(k).
- **Roth IRA**: contributions never deductible, but growth and qualified withdrawals tax-free. Income phase-out begins at ~$146k single / $230k married (2024).
- **Backdoor Roth**: high earners can contribute to a Traditional IRA then convert to Roth. Watch for the pro-rata rule.

## HSA - health savings account

- Available only with a high-deductible health plan (HDHP).
- **The triple tax advantage**:
  1. Contributions are pre-tax.
  2. Growth is tax-free.
  3. Qualified medical withdrawals are tax-free.
- Treat as a stealth retirement account. Pay current medical bills out of pocket if possible, save receipts, and reimburse yourself decades later - meanwhile the HSA compounds tax-free.
- After 65, withdrawals for any purpose are taxed as income (no penalty), making the HSA equivalent to a Traditional IRA at worst.

## Recommended priority order

1. **401(k) up to the full employer match** - free money.
2. **HSA to limit** if eligible - best tax treatment of any account.
3. **Roth IRA to limit** ($7,000) - tax-free growth, contribution withdrawals available anytime.
4. **Max 401(k)** to $23,000 - large amount of tax-deferred space.
5. **Mega backdoor Roth** if your plan allows after-tax contributions + in-plan conversion.
6. **Taxable brokerage** for goals before retirement; favor index funds for tax efficiency.

## Common mistakes

- **Leaving the match on the table** - single largest mistake.
- **Roth vs. Traditional confusion** - generally Roth if you expect higher tax rates in retirement; Traditional otherwise. Most 25–35 year olds favor Roth.
- **Cashing out a 401(k) when changing jobs** - rolls into a high tax + 10% penalty. Always roll to an IRA or new 401(k).
- **High-fee target-date funds** - check expense ratios; switch to index funds if your plan offers them.
