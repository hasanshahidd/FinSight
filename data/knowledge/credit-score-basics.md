# Credit Score Basics

A credit score is a 3-digit number lenders use to predict the probability you'll repay debt. The two dominant models are **FICO** (300–850) and **VantageScore** (300–850, slightly different inputs).

## The five factors of FICO

1. **Payment history (35%)** - on-time payments. A single 30-day late can drop a score 60–110 points and stay on report 7 years.
2. **Amounts owed / utilization (30%)** - credit card balances divided by limits. Aim for **under 10%** utilization, never above 30%. Reported on the statement date, not the due date.
3. **Length of credit history (15%)** - average age of accounts. Don't close old cards if they're free.
4. **Credit mix (10%)** - having a variety (revolving + installment) helps slightly.
5. **New credit (10%)** - hard inquiries cost ~5 points each, decay in 1 year, fall off in 2.

## What to do at each tier

- **Below 670 (Fair/Poor)**: focus on payment history. Set autopay for at least the minimum on every account. Pay down the highest-utilization card first.
- **670–740 (Good)**: optimize utilization. Pay the statement balance to ~5%. Avoid new applications unless needed.
- **740+ (Very Good / Excellent)**: maintain. Above ~760 you qualify for the best mortgage rates; further gains have minimal financial impact.

## Common mistakes

- **Closing old cards.** Drops average age and total available credit (raises utilization).
- **Maxing a 0% promo card.** Even at 0% APR, high utilization tanks the score for as long as the balance reports.
- **Missing one payment.** The single most damaging mistake. Always autopay minimums.
- **Co-signing.** You're 100% responsible. The other person's mistakes show on *your* report.

## Where to check

- **annualcreditreport.com** - free reports from all three bureaus, weekly. Official US site.
- **Credit Karma / Experian app** - free VantageScore (slightly different from FICO but tracks the same trends).
- **Many credit cards now provide a free FICO score** in their app monthly.

Soft pulls (you checking yourself) do **not** affect the score.
