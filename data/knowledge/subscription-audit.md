# How to Audit Subscriptions and Recurring Charges

Subscription creep is one of the most common silent budget killers. The average US household has ~12 paid subscriptions and overpays by an estimated $200–400/month due to forgotten or duplicate services.

## The 4-step audit

1. **List every recurring charge** from the last 90 days of transactions. Look for charges that repeat monthly or annually with the same merchant.
2. **Tag each one**: *use weekly*, *use monthly*, *use less than monthly*, *forgot I had it*.
3. **Cancel anything in the bottom two tiers.** If you don't remember it, you don't need it. If you use it less than monthly, ask whether a one-time rental, library version, or shared family plan would suffice.
4. **For the rest, hunt the price.** Annual plans often save 15–20% over monthly. Bundles (e.g., Disney+/Hulu) replace separate subs. Loyalty discounts are often available if you call to cancel.

## Categories to scrutinize first

- **Streaming video** — typical household: Netflix + Hulu + Disney+ + HBO Max + Prime. Few watch all five regularly. Rotate: subscribe one month, watch a season, cancel, rotate.
- **Music** — Spotify, Apple Music, SiriusXM, YouTube Premium. Most people only use one heavily.
- **Cloud storage** — overlapping iCloud, Google One, Dropbox, OneDrive plans.
- **News & reading** — multiple paywalled newspapers / magazines often duplicate coverage.
- **Software / apps** — productivity, photo editors, language apps. Many have free tiers that cover 80%+ of use.
- **Gym / fitness apps** — gym + Peloton + Apple Fitness + ClassPass. Pick one.
- **Insurance / warranties** — extended warranties on phones and electronics rarely pay out; identity-theft insurance is often duplicated by credit cards.

## The "calendar reminder" trick

For free trials and annual subscriptions, set a calendar reminder for **2 days before renewal**. Decide *then* if the service earned another year. Default to canceling and re-subscribing if you miss it — reactivation friction is the point.

## Tools

Most major banks now offer subscription tracking in their app. Third-party tools (Rocket Money, Trim) can negotiate lower rates and find hidden subscriptions, but they take a percentage of savings. Doing the audit manually once per quarter usually beats them.
