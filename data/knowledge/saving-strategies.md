# Practical Saving Strategies

Saving more is mostly behavioral, not informational. Most people know they should save; the question is making it automatic and frictionless.

## Automate first, optimize later

The single highest-impact change is to **make saving the default action**. Set up an automatic transfer from checking to savings/investments on the day after payday. The amount that doesn't reach your checking account doesn't get spent.

- Start with whatever you can — even $50/month.
- Increase the automation by 1% of income every 3 months until you hit your target rate.
- Use **separate accounts at separate banks** if you tend to "borrow" from savings.

## Pay yourself first

Treat savings as a non-negotiable bill — same priority as rent. Common ladder:

1. **Capture employer 401(k) match** (instant 50–100% return).
2. **High-interest debt payoff** (>7% APR) — guaranteed return.
3. **Emergency fund** to 3–6 months.
4. **Roth IRA** to limit ($7,000 in 2024, $7,500 if 50+).
5. **Max 401(k)** to limit ($23,000).
6. **Taxable brokerage** for goals beyond retirement.

## Cut the big three before the small ones

In rank order of leverage:

1. **Housing** (~30% of budget). Refinancing, downsizing, or moving for a job has more impact than any latte.
2. **Transportation** (~15%). Reliable used car > new car. Insurance shop-around saves $300–800/yr.
3. **Food** (~10–15%). Groceries-vs-dining is the single biggest variable category for most households.

A $200/month dining cut over 10 years at 8% return ≈ **$36,000**. Not negligible, but smaller than the housing decision.

## Friction techniques

- **Remove saved cards from shopping sites.** Re-entering details breaks impulse.
- **Two-day rule.** Add anything non-essential to a "wishlist" for 48 hours before buying.
- **Subscription audit quarterly.** The average household has 4–5 forgotten subscriptions worth $30–80/mo.
- **Cash for variable spending.** Studies show people spend 12–18% less when paying with physical cash vs. card.

## When you have unstable income

Build a **buffer fund** of 1 month's expenses *separate* from your emergency fund. Pay yourself a fixed "salary" from that buffer; let income land in a holding account first.
