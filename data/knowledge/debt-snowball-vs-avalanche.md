# Debt Payoff: Snowball vs. Avalanche

If you carry multiple debts, two well-known strategies compete for "fastest payoff": the **snowball** and the **avalanche**.

## The Avalanche method

Pay minimums on all debts. Direct every extra dollar to the debt with the **highest interest rate**. When that's gone, roll its payment into the next-highest-rate debt. Mathematically optimal - minimizes total interest paid.

## The Snowball method

Pay minimums on all debts. Direct every extra dollar to the debt with the **smallest balance**. When that's gone, roll its payment into the next-smallest. Slightly more total interest, but each payoff is a quick visible win.

## Which one to pick

The avalanche saves money. The snowball produces motivation. Behavioral economists have shown that **people who use the snowball are more likely to actually finish** because early wins reinforce the behavior. If your debts are similar in size or your balances are large, the avalanche's interest savings are substantial. If you have one or two small debts that could be eliminated quickly, snowballing those first and then switching to avalanche order on the larger debts is a reasonable hybrid.

## Both methods require the same prerequisites

1. Stop adding new debt - close or hide the credit cards.
2. Build a $1,000 starter emergency fund first; otherwise the next surprise expense becomes new debt.
3. List all debts with: balance, minimum payment, APR, and lender.
4. Find your "extra" - every dollar above minimums you can throw at debt.

## High-interest debt comes first regardless of method

Anything above ~10% APR (most credit cards, payday loans, some personal loans) should be a top priority over investing beyond a 401(k) match. The guaranteed return on paying off 22% APR debt beats nearly every public-market investment.
